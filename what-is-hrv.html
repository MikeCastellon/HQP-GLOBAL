Understanding Heart Rate Variability

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) refers to the variation in time between successive heartbeats. Contrary to what one might assume, a healthy heart does not beat with perfect regularity — in fact, greater variation between heartbeats is a marker of a healthy, responsive autonomic nervous system.

HRV is controlled by the autonomic nervous system (ANS), which has two branches: the sympathetic (fight-or-flight) and parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) systems. The balance between these two systems is reflected in HRV measurements and provides a powerful, non-invasive window into a patient's physiological resilience, stress response, and overall health.

Key HRV Parameters

Parameters Measured by CRIS GOLD™

CRIS GOLD™ evaluates all major time-domain and frequency-domain HRV parameters, cross-referenced with validated research.

SDNN

Standard Deviation of NN Intervals

Overall HRV measure reflecting total autonomic nervous system variability. A strong predictor of cardiovascular health and longevity.

RMSSD

Root Mean Square of Successive Differences

Primary indicator of parasympathetic activity and vagal tone. Strongly associated with recovery, sleep quality, and stress resilience.

LF Power

Low Frequency Power

Reflects a mix of sympathetic and parasympathetic influences. Associated with baroreflex sensitivity and blood pressure regulation.

HF Power

High Frequency Power

Primarily reflects parasympathetic (vagal) activity. Linked to respiratory sinus arrhythmia and parasympathetic nervous system regulation.

LF/HF Ratio

Sympathovagal Balance

The ratio of low to high frequency power — a key marker of sympathovagal balance and the body's stress/recovery state.

VLF Power

Very Low Frequency Power

Associated with thermoregulation, renin-angiotensin system activity, and intrinsic cardiac regulation. Strong mortality predictor.

TP

Total Power

Sum of all frequency bands. Reflects overall autonomic nervous system activity and global health status.

SI

Stress Index (Baevsky)

A composite measure of regulatory system tension. Elevated SI indicates high sympathetic dominance and reduced adaptive reserve.

pNN50

Percentage of NN50

Percentage of successive heartbeat intervals differing by more than 50ms. Another marker of parasympathetic activity.
